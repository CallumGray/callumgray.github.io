K.O Heatmaps (Python)

In the popular competitive fighting game "Super Smash Bros. Melee", a player gains damage (%) each time they are attacked. The higher their percentage, the further they get launched by subsequent attacks. Once a player is knocked out of the bounds of the screen, it results in a K.O, with 4 K.Os resulting in a victory.

Knowledge of exactly when an attack will K.O massively improves a player's judgement of risk-reward, and as a result this app was built. It creates heatmaps showing what percentage an attack will ensure a K.O on a character at every position on the stage.

Formulae for a character's knockback after being hit is plotted as their on-screen position for each frame (or 1/60th of a second). All algorithms were written in Python, with plots drawn using the libraries MatPlotLib and Seaborn.

Pokémon Team Builder (Python / SQL)

A team builder for the fan-made game "Pokémon Infinite Fusion".

In this game the player has access to over 500 pokemon, with the option to fuse any two of them together to form a unique pokemon. This involves mixing their types, stats, abilities and moves, giving an enormous amount of options when forming a team.

This team builder looks at available pokemon the player has access to at their stage of the game, and given their current team, will recommend the best fusions possible to synergise with what they already have.

The process of building the app involved thorough data-cleaning, alongside complex Python algorithms and tough SQL optimisation to work with such large amounts of data effectively.
